Accounting Software Module Reference Guide Detailed overview of each module — purpose, functionality, and required data fields CUSTOMER ACCOUNTS 

1. Customer Data Customer Data is the master record that stores all essential information about each customer. It serves as the foundation for all customer-related transactions throughout the software. Purpose: To maintain a centralised database of all customers, enabling quick access during invoicing, quotations, and payment tracking. Customer Name Full legal name or trading name of the customer Customer Code Unique alphanumeric identifier assigned to the customer Contact Person Name of the primary point of contact at the customer's organisation Phone / Mobile Contact numbers for communication and follow-up Email Address Email for sending invoices, statements, and correspondence Billing Address Full postal address for invoicing and legal documentation Shipping Address Delivery address if different from billing address GSTIN GST Identification Number for tax compliance and GSTR filing PAN Number Permanent Account Number for income tax records Payment Terms Agreed credit period (e.g., 30 days, 60 days, immediate) Credit Limit Maximum outstanding balance allowed for this customer Currency Default transaction currency (INR or foreign currency) Bank Details Customer's bank account details for refunds or transfers Opening Balance Any outstanding balance as of the system start date Customer Group Category or segment the customer belongs to (e.g., Retail, Wholesale) 

2. Quotation Preparation Quotation Preparation allows the business to create formal price offers for potential or existing customers before a sale is confirmed. It is a pre-sales document. Purpose: To provide customers with a formal offer outlining the goods or services, quantities, and prices — which can later be converted into a sales invoice. Quotation Number Unique system-generated or manual reference number Quotation Date Date on which the quotation is prepared Valid Until Expiry date of the quotation offer Customer Name Linked from Customer Data master record Billing Address Auto-populated from customer master Item / Service Description of the goods or services being quoted HSN / SAC Code Harmonised System Nomenclature or Service Accounting Code for GST Quantity Number of units being offered Unit of Measure Unit in which goods are measured (e.g., Nos, Kg, Litres) Unit Price Rate per unit before taxes Discount Any discount offered in percentage or fixed amount GST Rate Applicable CGST, SGST, or IGST rate GST Amount Calculated tax amount based on taxable value and rate Total Amount Grand total inclusive of all taxes and charges Terms & Conditions Payment terms, delivery timeline, and other conditions Narration / Notes Any special remarks or instructions for the customer 

3. Sales Invoice A Sales Invoice is the primary document raised by the business to bill a customer for goods sold or services rendered. It is a legally binding document and forms the basis for accounts receivable and GST reporting. Purpose: To record revenue, create a receivable entry in the customer's account, and generate data for GST returns (GSTR-1). Invoice Number Unique sequential invoice number (mandatory for GST compliance) Invoice Date Date of issue of the invoice Customer Name Linked from Customer Data master Place of Supply State of the customer — determines CGST/SGST vs IGST applicability Customer GSTIN Customer's GST number for B2B invoices Item / Service Description of goods or services supplied HSN / SAC Code Classification code for GST purposes Quantity Units of goods or hours/units of service Unit Price Rate per unit before tax Discount Trade or cash discount if applicable Taxable Value Value after discount, on which tax is calculated CGST / SGST / IGST Tax amounts split by type based on supply location Total Invoice Value Final amount payable including all taxes Payment Terms Due date for payment based on agreed credit period Bank Details Your bank details printed on invoice for customer payment Linked Quotation Reference to original quotation if converted E-Invoice / IRN Invoice Reference Number for e-invoicing if applicable 

4. Debit Note (Customer) A Debit Note raised on a customer is used to increase the amount owed by the customer. It is typically issued when additional charges need to be billed after the original invoice. Purpose: To revise an earlier invoice upwards — for instance, to charge for additional freight, price corrections, or extra services not included in the original invoice. Debit Note Number Unique reference number for the debit note Debit Note Date Date on which the debit note is issued Customer Name Linked from customer master Original Invoice Ref Reference to the original invoice being revised Reason / Narration Explanation for issuing the debit note Item / Charge Description of the additional charge being levied Taxable Amount Value on which GST is to be applied GST Rate & Amount Applicable GST on the additional charge Total Debit Amount Net amount being debited to the customer's account 

5. Credit Note (Customer) A Credit Note issued to a customer reduces the amount owed by the customer. It is raised when goods are returned, when there is a billing error, or when a discount is given after invoicing. Purpose: To reverse or reduce a previously raised sales invoice — either partially or fully. It also adjusts GST liability in GSTR-

1. Credit Note Number Unique reference number for the credit note Credit Note Date Date of issue Customer Name Linked from customer master Original Invoice Ref Invoice against which the credit note is being raised Reason for Credit Sales return, billing error, discount, goodwill adjustment, etc. Item / Charge Goods returned or charge being reversed Quantity Returned Units of goods returned if applicable Taxable Amount Value being credited before tax GST Rate & Amount GST reversal corresponding to credit Total Credit Amount Net amount being credited to customer's account VENDOR ACCOUNTS 

1. Vendor Data Vendor Data is the master record for all suppliers from whom goods or services are procured. It is the equivalent of Customer Data on the purchase side. Purpose: To maintain a complete and accurate database of all vendors, enabling efficient purchase processing, payment tracking, and reconciliation. Vendor Name Full legal name or trading name of the supplier Vendor Code Unique alphanumeric identifier for the vendor Contact Person Primary point of contact at the vendor's organisation Phone / Mobile Contact numbers Email Address For purchase orders, correspondence, and remittances Address Full registered address of the vendor GSTIN Vendor's GST number — critical for ITC (Input Tax Credit) claims PAN Number For TDS deduction and income tax compliance Payment Terms Agreed payment period (e.g., 30 days net) Bank Details Vendor's account number, IFSC code, and bank name for payment Opening Balance Outstanding amount payable as of system start date Vendor Category Classification such as goods supplier, service provider, contractor Currency Default transaction currency TDS Applicable Whether TDS is deducted on payments to this vendor 

2. Vendor Invoice A Vendor Invoice (also called a Purchase Invoice) is raised when goods or services are received from a vendor and the vendor's bill is entered into the system. It creates a payable entry in the vendor's account. Purpose: To record purchases, account for Input Tax Credit (ITC) on GST paid, and track amounts payable to vendors. It feeds into GSTR-2B reconciliation. Vendor Invoice No. The invoice number as printed on the vendor's bill Vendor Invoice Date Date on vendor's original invoice Entry Date Date the invoice is recorded in the system Vendor Name Linked from vendor master Vendor GSTIN For ITC eligibility verification Place of Supply Determines CGST/SGST vs IGST treatment Item / Service Description of goods purchased or services received HSN / SAC Code Product or service classification code Quantity Units received Unit Price Rate per unit charged by vendor Taxable Value Value before GST CGST / SGST / IGST Tax amounts as per vendor's invoice Total Invoice Amount Grand total payable to vendor Expense Account GL account to which the expense is posted (e.g., Purchases, Office Expenses) Linked PO Purchase order reference if applicable Payment Due Date Based on vendor payment terms 

3. Debit Note (Vendor) A Debit Note raised on a vendor is issued by your business to the vendor, indicating that you are reducing the amount payable to them. This typically happens when goods are returned or when you find an overcharge. Purpose: To reverse or reduce a vendor invoice — such as in the case of purchase returns, quality rejections, or price corrections. Also reverses the ITC claimed. Debit Note Number Your unique reference number Debit Note Date Date of issue Vendor Name Linked from vendor master Original Invoice Ref Vendor invoice being reversed or reduced Reason Return of goods, damaged stock, price dispute, overcharge, etc. Item Returned Description of goods being returned Quantity Returned Number of units returned Taxable Value Value of goods being returned GST Reversal ITC to be reversed corresponding to the return Total Debit Amount Net reduction in payable to vendor 

4. Recurring Expense Recurring Expense is a feature that allows the system to automatically create vendor invoices or expense entries on a defined schedule for expenses that occur regularly. Purpose: To automate the entry of fixed periodic expenses such as rent, subscriptions, retainers, AMC charges, and utility bills — reducing manual effort and ensuring no expense is missed. Template Name A descriptive name for the recurring entry (e.g., Monthly Office Rent) Vendor Name Vendor to whom this expense is payable Expense Account GL account for posting (e.g., Rent Expense, Subscription Charges) Amount Fixed amount to be posted each period GST Rate If GST is applicable on the recurring expense Frequency Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Quarterly, or Annual Start Date First date from which recurrence begins End Date Date after which the recurrence stops (or indefinite) Next Due Date Auto-calculated date for the next entry Narration Description that will appear on each auto-generated entry Auto-Post Whether entry is posted automatically or requires manual approval BANKING 

1. Bank The Bank module manages all transactions related to bank accounts — including receipts from customers, payments to vendors, bank charges, interest credits, and inter-bank transfers. Purpose: To record all bank transactions, maintain an accurate bank ledger, and facilitate bank reconciliation by matching system entries with bank statements. Bank Account Name Name of the bank account (e.g., HDFC Current Account) Account Number Full bank account number IFSC Code Bank branch IFSC for NEFT/RTGS transactions Bank Name & Branch Name of bank and branch Opening Balance Balance as of the start date in the software Transaction Date Date of each bank transaction Transaction Type Receipt, Payment, Transfer, Bank Charge, Interest, etc. Party Name Customer or vendor involved in the transaction Reference / Cheque No. Cheque number, UTR, NEFT reference, or transaction ID Amount Transaction value Narration Description of the transaction purpose Linked Voucher Reference to the invoice or journal this payment settles Reconciliation Status Whether the entry has been matched against bank statement 

2. Cash The Cash module records all transactions involving physical cash — petty cash expenses, cash sales, cash receipts, and cash payments. It maintains the cash book of the business. Purpose: To track day-to-day cash movements accurately, maintain a running cash balance, and ensure proper accountability of petty cash and cash-in-hand. Cash Account Name Name of the cash account (e.g., Petty Cash, Main Cash) Opening Balance Cash balance at the start of the accounting period Transaction Date Date of the cash transaction Transaction Type Cash Receipt, Cash Payment, Petty Cash Expense Party Name Person or entity involved (if applicable) Amount Cash amount received or paid Expense / Income Head Account to which the transaction is posted Narration Description of the purpose (e.g., Office supplies purchased) Receipt / Voucher No. Internal voucher number for the cash transaction Approved By Name of the person authorising the cash transaction ACCOUNTANT 

1. Manual Journal A Manual Journal (or Journal Voucher) is used to record accounting entries that do not arise from standard sales, purchases, or banking transactions. It gives the accountant direct control over the general ledger. Purpose: To pass adjustment entries, rectification entries, depreciation, provisions, accruals, prepayments, and any other entries that require direct debit/credit posting to ledger accounts. Journal Number Unique system-generated or manual reference number Journal Date Date of the journal entry Journal Type General Journal, Adjustment, Rectification, Depreciation, etc. Debit Account Ledger account(s) to be debited Credit Account Ledger account(s) to be credited Debit Amount Amount to be debited — must equal total credit amount Credit Amount Amount to be credited — must balance with debit Narration Detailed description of the reason for the entry Supporting Reference Document or reference supporting the entry Prepared By Name of the staff entering the journal Approved By Name of the authorising person Cost Centre / Job Department or job allocation if applicable 

2. Recurring Journal Recurring Journal automates the creation of periodic journal entries that need to be posted on a regular schedule — similar to Recurring Expenses but for general ledger adjustments. Purpose: To automate entries like monthly depreciation, prepaid expense amortisation, accrued income recognition, and interest calculations without manual intervention each period. Template Name Descriptive name for the recurring journal (e.g., Monthly Depreciation) Debit Account Ledger account to be debited periodically Credit Account Ledger account to be credited periodically Amount Fixed amount to be posted each cycle Narration Description auto-applied to each entry Frequency Monthly, Quarterly, Half-yearly, Annual Start Date Date from which recurrence begins End Date Date after which recurrence stops Next Due Date System-calculated date of next posting Auto-Post Whether the entry posts automatically or requires confirmation OTHERS 

1. Transaction Locking Transaction Locking is a control feature that prevents modification or deletion of accounting entries after a specified date. It protects the integrity of completed and audited periods. Purpose: To lock closed accounting periods (monthly, quarterly, or annual) so that no accidental or unauthorised changes can be made to finalised transactions. Lock Date The date up to which transactions are locked (e.g., 31 March) Lock Type Full lock (no edits) or soft lock (restricted access with password) Applicable Modules Sales, Purchases, Banking, Journals — all or selective modules Password Override Optional password for administrators to temporarily override the lock Locked By Name of the administrator who applied the lock Lock Date Applied Date on which the lock was activated 

2. Chart of Accounts The Chart of Accounts (COA) is the complete list of all ledger accounts used in the accounting system. It is the backbone of the entire accounting structure. Purpose: To organise and classify all financial transactions into appropriate accounts — enabling accurate financial reporting, trial balance, and statutory compliance. Account Code Unique numerical or alphanumeric code for each account Account Name Descriptive name (e.g., Trade Receivables, Office Rent, Bank Charges) Account Type Assets, Liabilities, Income, Expense, or Equity Account Group Sub-classification (e.g., Current Assets, Fixed Assets, Direct Income) Parent Account Hierarchy — the group under which this account falls Opening Balance Balance at the start of the accounting period GST Applicable Whether GST transactions flow through this account TDS Applicable Whether TDS is applicable on transactions through this account Currency If the account is maintained in a foreign currency Account Status Active or Inactive 

3. Fixed Asset Register The Fixed Asset Register maintains a record of all long-term tangible assets owned by the business — such as machinery, vehicles, furniture, computers, and buildings. Purpose: To track the acquisition, depreciation, disposal, and current book value of all fixed assets, and to ensure compliance with accounting standards and income tax regulations. Asset Name Description of the asset (e.g., Dell Laptop, Toyota Innova) Asset Code Unique identification code for the asset Asset Category Plant & Machinery, Furniture & Fixtures, Vehicles, etc. Purchase Date Date of acquisition Purchase Price Original cost of acquisition including installation charges Vendor Name Supplier from whom the asset was purchased Invoice Reference Purchase invoice number Useful Life Expected years of usage Depreciation Method Straight Line Method (SLM) or Written Down Value (WDV) Depreciation Rate Annual percentage at which the asset depreciates Accumulated Depreciation Total depreciation charged to date Net Book Value Current value (Purchase Price minus Accumulated Depreciation) Asset Location Physical location of the asset (branch, department) Disposal Date Date on which the asset was sold or scrapped (if applicable) Disposal Value Amount received upon disposal 

4. Partners Account Partners Account tracks the capital contributions, drawings, profit sharing, and current accounts of partners in a partnership or LLP firm. Purpose: To maintain separate ledgers for each partner — recording their capital introduced, drawings taken, interest on capital, salary/commission due, and share of profits and losses. Partner Name Full name of the partner Partner Code Unique identifier for each partner's account Capital Account Fixed capital contributed by the partner Current Account Variable account tracking drawings, salary, interest, and profit share Profit Sharing Ratio Percentage or ratio in which profits are shared Interest on Capital Rate of interest allowed on capital balance Partner's Salary Fixed remuneration per period if applicable Drawings Amount withdrawn by the partner during the period Interest on Drawings Charge on drawings per partnership agreement Opening Balance Balance at start of accounting year Closing Balance Computed balance after all transactions for the period Job Register The Job Register is used to track income and expenses against specific jobs, projects, contracts, or assignments. It enables project-wise profitability analysis. Purpose: To monitor the financial performance of individual jobs or projects by linking all related revenues and costs to a job code — useful for contractors, service firms, and project-based businesses. Job Code Unique identifier for each job or project Job Name Descriptive name of the job or project Customer Name Client for whom the job is being executed Job Start Date Date when the job commenced Expected End Date Projected completion date Actual End Date Date on which the job was completed Contract Value Total agreed value or estimated revenue for the job Expenses Incurred All costs linked to the job (materials, labour, overheads) Invoices Raised Sales invoices linked to this job Job Status Open, In Progress, Completed, or Closed Cost Centre Department or cost centre linked to the job Gross Profit Revenue minus direct costs for the job REPORTS 

1. Profit and Loss Account The Profit and Loss (P&L) Account summarises all income and expenses over a defined period to show the net profit or net loss of the business. Key Fields: Date range, income heads (sales, other income), direct and indirect expense heads, gross profit, operating profit, and net profit/loss. 

2. Balance Sheet The Balance Sheet presents the financial position of the business at a specific point in time, showing assets, liabilities, and equity/capital. Key Fields: As-at date, fixed and current assets, current and long-term liabilities, capital accounts, reserves and surplus, and net worth. 

3. Trial Balance The Trial Balance lists all ledger accounts with their debit and credit balances to verify that the books are arithmetically balanced. Key Fields: Date range, account code, account name, opening balance, transactions during the period, and closing balance in debit/credit columns. 

4. Cash Flow Statement The Cash Flow Statement shows how cash moves in and out of the business across operating, investing, and financing activities over a period. Key Fields: Period, operating activities (receipts and payments), investing activities (asset purchases/sales), financing activities (loans, capital), and net change in cash. 

5. AR Ageing Summary The Accounts Receivable Ageing Summary categorises outstanding customer dues by how long they have been overdue. Key Fields: Customer name, invoice number and date, total outstanding, and buckets such as 0-30 days, 31-60 days, 61-90 days, and 90+ days overdue. 

6. AP Ageing Summary The Accounts Payable Ageing Summary categorises outstanding vendor payables by overdue period. Key Fields: Vendor name, invoice reference, total payable, and ageing buckets (0-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+ days) to identify urgent payments. 

7. Sales Register The Sales Register lists all sales invoices raised during a period with full details — used for sales analysis and GSTR-1 preparation. Key Fields: Invoice number and date, customer name, GSTIN, place of supply, taxable value, GST breakup (CGST/SGST/IGST), and total invoice value. 

8. Debit Note Register (Customer) Lists all debit notes raised on customers during a period — tracks additional charges billed and their GST implications. Key Fields: Debit note number and date, customer name, original invoice reference, reason, taxable value, GST amount, and net debit amount. 

9. Credit Note Register (Customer) Lists all credit notes issued to customers — used to track returns, discounts, and billing reversals, and to report in GSTR-

1. Key Fields: Credit note number and date, customer name, original invoice reference, reason for credit, taxable value, GST reversal, and net credit amount. 

10. Purchase Register The Purchase Register lists all vendor invoices recorded during a period — forms the basis for ITC claims and vendor analysis. Key Fields: Vendor invoice number and date, vendor name and GSTIN, item description, HSN/SAC, taxable value, CGST/SGST/IGST, and total invoice value. 

11. Debit Note (Vendor) Register Lists all debit notes raised on vendors (purchase return notes) — tracks ITC reversals and returns to suppliers. Key Fields: Debit note number and date, vendor name, original purchase invoice, goods returned, taxable value, GST reversed, and net debit to vendor. 

12. GSTR-1 Summary GSTR-1 is the monthly or quarterly outward supply return filed by GST-registered businesses. This report compiles all sales data in the prescribed format. Key Fields: B2B invoices (with customer GSTIN), B2C invoices, exports, credit/debit notes, nil-rated and exempt supplies, HSN-wise summary, and consolidated tax liability. 

13. GSTR-2B Summary GSTR-2B is the auto-drafted input tax credit statement generated from supplier filings. This report shows ITC available for claiming. Key Fields: Vendor GSTIN, invoice details, ITC available (CGST/SGST/IGST), ITC ineligible under Section 17(5), and reconciliation status with purchase register. 

14. GSTR Summary A consolidated GST summary report giving an overview of total output tax liability and input tax credit across all GST returns for the period. Key Fields: Total output GST (from GSTR-1), total ITC (from GSTR-2B), net GST payable, and period-wise comparison. 

15. Ledger Statement A Ledger Statement shows all debit and credit entries for a specific ledger account during a selected period, with running balance. Key Fields: Account name, date range, opening balance, date-wise transactions with narration and voucher reference, and closing balance. 

16. Job Register The Job Register report lists all jobs with their financial summary — revenues, costs, and profitability. Key Fields: Job code and name, customer, start and end dates, contract value, total expenses, invoices raised, and gross profit/loss per job. 

17. Job Wise Profit Report Provides a detailed profitability analysis for each job — breaking down income and expenditure to compute gross and net profit at the job level. Key Fields: Job code, income billed, cost breakdown by category (materials, labour, subcontract, overheads), gross profit, and profit margin percentage. 

18. Sales by Customer Analyses total sales made to each customer during a period — useful for identifying top customers and tracking buying patterns. Key Fields: Customer name, number of invoices, total taxable value, total GST, total invoice value, and percentage contribution to overall sales. 

19. Sales by Charges Breaks down sales by individual income heads or service charges — showing which products or services contribute most to revenue. Key Fields: Charge/item description, HSN/SAC code, total quantity sold, total taxable value, and GST for each charge or product category. 

20. Purchase by Vendor Summarises total purchases from each vendor over a period — used for vendor analysis, negotiation, and spend management. Key Fields: Vendor name, number of invoices, total taxable purchases, ITC claimed, total purchase value, and vendor's share of total purchases. 

21. Purchase by Charges Breaks down purchases by expense head or item category — showing where the business is spending its procurement budget. Key Fields: Expense head, item description, HSN/SAC, total quantity purchased, taxable value, ITC amount, and total expenditure per category. 

22. Day Book The Day Book (also called the Daily Transaction Report) lists all transactions entered in the system on a given date — across all modules. Key Fields: Date, voucher number, voucher type (sales, purchase, receipt, payment, journal), party name, amount, and account heads involved. 

23. Journal Report The Journal Report lists all manual journal entries passed during a selected period — useful for review and audit. Key Fields: Journal number and date, debit and credit accounts, amounts, narration, and the name of the user who created and approved the entry. 

24. Ledger Statement (Duplicate listing in report menu — same as report No. 15 above.) The Ledger Statement gives a full account history for any selected ledger account over a chosen date range. Key Fields: Account name, period selected, opening balance, chronological list of all transactions with dates, voucher references, narrations, debit and credit amounts, and closing balance.
